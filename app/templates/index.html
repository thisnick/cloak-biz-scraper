What you can ask it to do

Once it’s connected, you just talk to it in plain English. A few things to try:

Browse sites that block bots

Open a cloaked browser and navigate any site. With an Evomi proxy configured, it uses a residential exit; without one, it uses this server's datacenter connection and listing sites may block it.

  • “Open a browser, go to this listing, and tell me the asking price and cash flow.”
  • “Click into the third result and summarise the financials.”

An Evomi proxy is recommended for listing sites — without one, your server IP looks like a datacenter and may be blocked.

Scrape a page of search results

Point it at a search-results page and it pulls every listing off it — title, location, price, cash flow, link — straight back to you. No Notion needed.

  • “Scrape this BizBuySell search:” — paste the URL from your address bar after applying your filters.
  • “Search BizBuySell for California businesses under $1M cash flow and scrape the results.”
…and keep them in Notion
  • “Scrape that search and add any new ones to my Notion, skipping duplicates.”
  • “Save this listing’s full page to my Notion:” — paste a listing URL.

Needs Notion — connect it and pick a database in Settings first.

Get a CDP URL for your own tools developers

Launch a cloaked browser and get back a CDP URL you can drive from Claude Code, Codex, Playwright, or agent-browser — through your proxy when configured, or through the server directly. The browser stays up ~15 min idle (60 min max); ask again for a fresh URL to reconnect.

  • “Launch a browser and give me the CDP URL.”
  • Then point Playwright (connectOverCDP) or agent-browser at that URL and drive it yourself.
